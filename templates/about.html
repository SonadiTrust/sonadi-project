Our Story

Rooted in Kindness, Growing with Purpose

“The greatness of a nation can be judged by the way its animals are treated.” – Mahatma Gandhi

Founded in 2001 under the visionary leadership of Mrs. Bandana Sengupta and Dr. Vijay Kumar, Sonadi Charitable Trust began its journey in Chittaranjan Park, New Delhi, with a single mission — to bring compassionate care to the lives of voiceless animals.

From modest beginnings, Sonadi has grown into a leading shelter and treatment facility for injured, abandoned, and mistreated street animals. With unwavering commitment, our veterinarians, para-vets, caretakers, and volunteers have transformed thousands of lives.

Key programs include the Animal Birth Control Program, aimed at reducing human-animal conflict, controlling population, and eliminating rabies. We run a state-of-the-art unit for sterilization surgeries on dogs and cats, ensuring quality post-op care.

Rescue operations are powered by medical ambulances for ill or abandoned animals. We also conduct anti-rabies vaccination drives, in collaboration with community groups and local volunteers.

So far, Sonadi has successfully performed over 2 Lakh ABC surgeries, treated countless animals, and provided lifelong shelter to many who can no longer survive on the streets.

At Sonadi, our vision is to create a future where no animal suffers alone. We aim to build a compassionate medical facility that ensures healing with dignity and love.

Through adoption campaigns, awareness drives, and community outreach, we’re building a culture of compassion — one paw at a time.
